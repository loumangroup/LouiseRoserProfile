PROJECT PROPOSAL

OVERVIEW

PopSwap is a concept for an app for iOS and Android. It is intended to be used as an open marketplace for trading of second hand clothes and accessories, with no exchange of money, only goods. The app will allow everyday people to find others to trade with, to continue changing their wardrobes without adding to mass clothing wastage or contributing to fast fashion, all while saving money and rehoming unused items.

MOTIVATION

Even the least fashionable among us will have noticed the changing landscape of the fashion industry. The increased use of internet based fast-fashion brands has caused the trend cycle to accelerate, with online fashion giant Shein adding an average of 2,000 new products per day in 2021(Chen, 2021). On average, Australians acquire 27 kilograms of new clothing every year, with 23 kilograms of their old clothing ending up in a landfill (Clothing Textiles Waste, 2021).

The industry is a sustainability nightmare, but the hope comes from the increased demand for second hand clothing, with Australian marketplace gumtree citing that clothing, shoes and accessories as their most popular items to sell, an increase of over 500% in sales since 2011(Gumtree Second Hand Economy Report, 2019). With the creation of PopSwap, Australians can trade their unwanted clothing, satisfying their need for new items without contributing to the overproduction of new pieces, and preventing their old pieces from ending up in landfills.

DESCRIPTION

The app will be free for cross platform download on iOS or Android. Users can list the items they have grown out of or grown tired of on the app. Each item will be graded into a categorical value system, and a type of item, i.e. dress, pants, necklace. They will be able to give the item a brief description describing its quality and condition.

The value system takes away exact dollar value from the listing that other users will see, so as to deter from too much value comparison, encouraging a more flexible marketplace. For example, an item the owner values at $45 might be listed as Category C, while an item valued at $80 might be Category E. This way users will have a rough indication of how much something is worth, and can inform how much they want to trade for a particular item, without making it necessary for trades to be exactly equal in value.

The trades themselves will be the onus of the traders, and the map function of the app will show roughly how far a specific item is from you. This way, people who only want to trade locally can find people in their area to trade with. Trades will be encouraged to be done in public places, but as any open marketplace, it is up to the discretion of the traders. A possibility for further development could be nation wide trading, but the burden of postage would be on the traders, and would require possible success of a local version of the app.

IN APP TASKBAR
  • PROFILE - where users can update their details and location, list new items or edit listings
  • MAP - an interactive map showing listed items near them, within a certain radius. Take for example, the map on a booking website, showing the hotels in an area. You can move around the map as you please, to see trades around you.
  • SEARCH - Where users can search particular categories of items.
  • TRADES - Where users can access active trade offers and messages with other users.
TRADING PROCESS
  • User1 can select an item they are interested in, and notify the owner, User2.
  • User2 can then see this selection, and they can look through the catalogue of User1's listed items.
  • User2 can select the item/s in User1's profile they would be willing to trade for.
  • User1 would be notified, and can agree to the trade, or continue negotiating with User2.

To incentivise honest trades and behaviour on the app, each user will have a star rating on their profile, which will be crowdsourced from those they have traded with before. Take the example with User1 and User2. When they log the trade as completed, they will be prompted to rate each other. Ratings should take into consideration that the quality of the item matches its description and the conduct of the user at the swap.

TOOLS AND TECHNOLOGIES

The app would be cross platform, but requiring a fairly large amount of processing power, to display photos and messages. The app would be built using an existing framework that allows the builder to utilise functions such as push notifications, profile creation and access to databases. BuildFire JS is an option for this procedure. It is an open source app builder which allows you to use html or JavaScript on top of its existing platforms, to minimise the amount of functions that need to be built from scratch.

The app will need the ability to store and display photos, profiles, messages and trade history, so a large server would be necessary. There may be the possibility to put a cap on how many items a user can have listed, to cut down on stored data.

SKILLS REQUIRED

The app will need to be incredibly user friendly, so a good understanding is UX Design is necessary. The interface needs to be accessible and aesthetically pleasing, so Front End Development is important to the process. To build the app on an existing open source platform will reduce the overall knowledge needed by the builders, but to implement all features of the app properly, the builder will need fairly comprehensive knowledge of the framework, and the web based language (i.e. JavaScript) they will use to build the remaining functions, and customise the app to be a unique interface.

A cloud based server will need to be reserved, most likely through a third party company, so there will be operating costs to keep the app viable.

OUTCOME

A successful implementation of this project will create a space for people to be more conscious of their wardrobe, their consumption and environmental impact in regards to fashion. The app will allow people to continue to update or change their style without needing to spend money or increase the number of items they already own, and deter people from buying from fast fashion websites. The app will encourage people to alter their views on consumption, and this change in attitudes can slowly but surely affect large companies, forcing movement in the industry landscape. Ideally, the app will slow the acceleration of waste in the fashion industry, and motivate people to be more mindful of their personal impact.
